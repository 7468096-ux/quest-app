# Labor Code of the Philippines — Key Provisions
# Presidential Decree No. 442 (as amended)
# Knowledge Base for AbugadoAI

---

## OVERVIEW
The Labor Code of the Philippines (PD 442, 1974) governs employment relationships, working conditions, wages, termination, and labor dispute resolution. It is administered by the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) and adjudicated by the National Labor Relations Commission (NLRC).

---

## PART I: PRE-EMPLOYMENT

### Employment Status
**Regular Employees:**
- An employee who has been engaged to perform activities necessary or desirable to the employer's business for at least **one year** (whether continuous or broken) is deemed regular
- Regular employees have security of tenure — cannot be dismissed without just or authorized cause

**Probationary Employees:**
- Cannot exceed **6 months**
- Must be informed at time of engagement of the standards for regularization
- If allowed to work beyond 6 months, automatically becomes regular

**Casual Employees:**
- Engaged for work not necessary or desirable to the business
- Entitled to regular status after 1 year of service for the same activities

**Project Employees:**
- Hired for a specific project with a definite duration
- Must be informed of project end date at time of hiring

**Seasonal Employees:**
- Work is dependent on seasons (e.g., sugar harvest)
- Return each season and are considered regular seasonal employees

**Fixed-Term Employees:**
- Employment ends on a specified date
- Must be voluntary and entered without coercion

### Contracting and Labor-Only Contracting
- **Legitimate contracting**: contractor has capital, tools, and exercises control over employees
- **Labor-only contracting** (prohibited): contractor merely supplies workers with no capital or control — workers are deemed direct employees of the principal

## PART II: CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

### Working Hours
- Normal hours of work: **8 hours per day** (Art. 83)
- Hours of work include all time an employee is required to be on duty or at the prescribed workplace
- Meal break of at least **60 minutes** is not compensable (Art. 85)
- Night shift work: between **10:00 PM and 6:00 AM**

### Overtime Pay
- Work beyond 8 hours: **+25% of hourly rate** (regular day)
- Overtime on rest day or special holiday: **+30% of hourly rest day rate**
- Emergency overtime may be required by employer (Art. 89)

### Holiday Pay
| Day Type | Pay Rate |
|----------|----------|
| Regular Holiday (no work) | 100% of daily wage |
| Regular Holiday (worked) | 200% of daily wage |
| Special Non-Working Holiday (no work) | No pay (unless CBA/company practice) |
| Special Non-Working Holiday (worked) | 130% of daily wage |

**Regular Holidays (12 days):** New Year's Day, Maundy Thursday, Good Friday, Araw ng Kagitingan, Labor Day, Independence Day, National Heroes Day, Eid'l Fitr, Eid'l Adha, Bonifacio Day, Christmas Day, Rizal Day

**Special Non-Working Holidays:** Christmas Eve, New Year's Eve, Ninoy Aquino Day, All Saints Day, All Souls Day, plus local holidays

### Rest Day
- Every employee is entitled to a rest period of not less than **24 consecutive hours** after 6 consecutive workdays (Art. 91)
- Employer determines the rest day (usually Sunday, or Friday for Muslim employees)
- If required to work on rest day: **+30% of daily rate**

### Night Shift Differential
- Employees working between **10:00 PM and 6:00 AM** are entitled to **10% additional pay** for hours worked during this period (Art. 86)

### Leave Benefits
**Service Incentive Leave (SIL):**
- After 1 year of service: **5 days paid leave** per year (Art. 95)
- Can be used for vacation or sick leave
- Unutilized SIL can be converted to cash at end of year

**Maternity Leave (RA 11210 — 105-Day Maternity Leave Law):**
- 105 days paid maternity leave for live childbirth
- 60 days for miscarriage or emergency termination
- Additional 30 days unpaid (optional extension)
- 7 days can be transferred to father (Paternity Leave under RA 8187: 7 days paid, up to 4 deliveries)

**Paternity Leave (RA 8187):**
- 7 days paid leave for married male employees
- First 4 deliveries only

**Parental Leave (Solo Parents — RA 8972):**
- 7 days additional parental leave per year for solo parents

**VAWC Leave (RA 9262 — Anti-VAWC Act):**
- 10 days paid leave for women who are victims of violence

**Special Leave (Magna Carta of Women, RA 9710):**
- 2 months special leave after surgery due to gynecological disorders

### 13th Month Pay (PD 851)
- **All rank-and-file employees** who worked for at least 1 month are entitled to 13th month pay
- Amount: 1/12 of total basic salary earned during the calendar year
- Must be paid **on or before December 24**
- Exempt: government employees, managerial employees, those paid purely by results, domestic workers

## PART III: WAGES

### Minimum Wage
- Set by Regional Tripartite Wages and Productivity Boards (RTWPB) per region
- **NCR (Metro Manila) minimum wage** (as of 2024): ₱610/day (non-agricultural)
- Other regions have lower rates; check NWPC website for current rates

### Wage Deductions
- **Allowed**: tax withholding, SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG premiums, union dues (with authorization), amounts owed to employer (with written consent)
- **NOT allowed**: unreasonable deductions, fines/penalties not authorized by law

### Wage Distortion
- Occurs when a wage order narrows the gap between pay grades
- Employer must restore the differential with affected employees

## PART IV: TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

### Just Causes (Art. 297) — employee's fault
Employer may dismiss for:
1. **Serious misconduct** or willful disobedience of lawful orders
2. **Gross and habitual neglect of duties**
3. **Fraud or willful breach of trust**
4. **Commission of a crime** against the employer or immediate family
5. **Other analogous causes**

### Authorized Causes (Art. 298 & 299) — business necessity
1. **Retrenchment** (reduction of costs to prevent losses)
2. **Redundancy** (position becomes excess due to reorganization)
3. **Closure/cessation of business**
4. **Disease** (employee's illness is incurable and poses risk to co-workers)

### Due Process for Dismissal
**For Just Causes — Twin Notice Rule:**
1. **First Notice (Charge Sheet):** Written notice specifying the acts/omissions, with a minimum **5-day period** to submit a written explanation
2. **Hearing/Conference** (optional but recommended)
3. **Second Notice (Decision):** Written notice informing employee of the decision to dismiss

**For Authorized Causes:**
- At least **30 days written notice** to the employee AND the DOLE
- No charge sheet or hearing required (no fault of employee)

### Separation Pay
| Cause | Separation Pay |
|-------|---------------|
| Redundancy | 1 month pay per year of service (or 1 month pay, whichever is higher) |
| Retrenchment | 1/2 month pay per year of service (or 1 month pay, whichever is higher) |
| Closure (not due to serious losses) | 1/2 month pay per year of service |
| Closure (due to serious losses) | Not required |
| Disease | 1/2 month pay per year of service |
| Reinstatement not feasible (illegal dismissal) | 1 month per year of service (in lieu of reinstatement) |

### Illegal Dismissal
- Dismissal without just or authorized cause, OR without due process
- Remedies:
  1. **Reinstatement** to former position without loss of seniority
  2. **Full back wages** from time of dismissal to reinstatement (no deductions)
  3. **Separation pay** (if reinstatement is no longer feasible)
- File complaint with **NLRC** (National Labor Relations Commission) or **DOLE**

### Resignation
- No specific form required, but written resignation is best practice
- If **forced/constructive dismissal** (employer made working conditions unbearable to force resignation), employee can claim illegal dismissal
- After resignation, employee is entitled to final pay within **30 days** (DOLE Labor Advisory)

### Final Pay
Final pay must include:
- Last salary for days worked
- Pro-rated 13th month pay
- Cash conversion of unused SIL
- Tax refund (if overpaid)
- Other benefits per contract or CBA

## PART V: SOCIAL BENEFITS

### SSS (Social Security System) — Private Sector
- Employer and employee contributions mandatory
- Benefits: sickness, maternity, disability, retirement, death, funeral
- Employer contribution: ~9.5% of monthly salary credit; Employee: ~4.5%

### PhilHealth (Philippine Health Insurance Corporation)
- Mandatory for all employees
- Covers hospitalization, surgeries, outpatient consultations
- Total premium: 5% of monthly basic salary (employer 2.5% + employee 2.5%)

### Pag-IBIG Fund (HDMF — Home Development Mutual Fund)
- Mandatory provident fund and housing loan program
- Monthly contribution: 2% of monthly salary (capped at ₱200/month employee share; employer matches)
- After 24 months contributions: eligible for housing, calamity, and multi-purpose loans

## PART VI: SPECIAL WORKERS

### Domestic Workers (Kasambahay — RA 10361)
- Minimum wage: ₱6,000/month (NCR); ₱5,000/month (chartered cities); ₱4,500/month (others)
- Entitled to: 24-hour rest per week, 5-day SIL, 13th month pay
- Employer must register with SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG
- Written employment contract required

### Homeworkers (RA 12023, if applicable)
- Workers doing work at home for an employer
- Entitled to minimum wage and benefits

### Night Workers (RA 10151)
- Workers in establishments operating between 10 PM and 6 AM
- Entitled to: night shift differential, mandatory health assessment, transfer rights for pregnant workers

### OFWs (Overseas Filipino Workers)
- Governed by **Migrant Workers and Overseas Filipinos Act (RA 10022)**
- POEA (Philippine Overseas Employment Administration, now DMW) regulates deployment
- Standard employment contracts ensure minimum standards regardless of host country
- OWWA provides welfare assistance, repatriation, and reintegration programs
- Illegal recruiter = criminal offense (imprisonment up to life imprisonment if large-scale)

## PART VII: LABOR DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### SENA (Single Entry Approach)
- Mandatory 30-day conciliation-mediation at DOLE before formal complaint filing
- Fast, free, and informal resolution

### NLRC (National Labor Relations Commission)
- Handles illegal dismissal, money claims, unfair labor practices
- Decisions can be appealed to the Court of Appeals

### DOLE (Department of Labor and Employment)
- Handles labor standards violations (non-payment of wages, overtime, 13th month pay)
- DOLE inspection can order payment of wage deficiencies

### Prescriptive Periods for Labor Claims
| Claim | Period |
|-------|--------|
| Illegal dismissal | 4 years from dismissal |
| Money claims (wages, overtime, etc.) | 3 years from when they became due |
| Unfair labor practice | 1 year from the date of the ULP act |
