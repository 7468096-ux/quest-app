## BIOLOGY

### Cell Structure and Function
**Prokaryotic cells** (bacteria): no nucleus, no membrane-bound organelles
**Eukaryotic cells** (animals, plants, fungi, protists): has nucleus and organelles

Key organelles:
| Organelle | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| Nucleus | Contains DNA; controls cell activities |
| Cell membrane | Regulates what enters/exits cell |
| Cell wall (plants, fungi) | Provides structure and rigidity |
| Mitochondria | Energy production (ATP); "powerhouse of the cell" |
| Chloroplast (plants only) | Photosynthesis; captures sunlight |
| Ribosomes | Protein synthesis |
| Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER) | Rough ER: protein synthesis; Smooth ER: lipid synthesis |
| Golgi apparatus | Sorts and packages proteins for transport |
| Vacuole | Storage; large in plant cells |
| Lysosomes | Digestion of cellular waste |

### Cell Processes
**Photosynthesis (in chloroplasts):**
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- Occurs in plants, algae, cyanobacteria
- Light reactions: in thylakoid membranes (produces ATP and NADPH)
- Calvin Cycle (dark reactions): in stroma (produces glucose)

**Cellular Respiration (in mitochondria):**
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + 38 ATP (energy)
- Glycolysis: in cytoplasm
- Krebs Cycle: in mitochondrial matrix
- Electron Transport Chain: inner mitochondrial membrane

**Cell Division:**
- **Mitosis**: produces 2 identical daughter cells (growth, repair)
  Phases: PMAT — Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase
- **Meiosis**: produces 4 cells with half chromosomes (gametes/sex cells)
  - Meiosis I: separates homologous chromosomes
  - Meiosis II: separates sister chromatids
  - Result: 4 haploid cells (n) from 1 diploid cell (2n)

### Genetics and Heredity
- **DNA** → transcription → **mRNA** → translation → **Protein**
- DNA bases: Adenine-Thymine (A-T), Guanine-Cytosine (G-C)
- RNA bases: Adenine-Uracil (A-U), Guanine-Cytosine (G-C)
- Dominant allele (B): masks recessive allele (b)
- Genotype: genetic makeup (BB, Bb, bb)
- Phenotype: physical expression (brown eyes, blue eyes)
- Punnett Square: tool to predict offspring genotype ratios
  - Bb × Bb → BB:Bb:bb = 1:2:1 ratio → phenotype 3 dominant : 1 recessive

### Human Body Systems
| System | Function | Key Organs |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Circulatory | Transport blood, nutrients, oxygen | Heart, blood vessels, blood |
| Respiratory | Gas exchange (O₂ in, CO₂ out) | Lungs, trachea, diaphragm |
| Digestive | Break down and absorb food | Stomach, intestines, liver |
| Nervous | Control and coordinate body | Brain, spinal cord, nerves |
| Endocrine | Hormone regulation | Pituitary, thyroid, adrenals |
| Immune | Defense against pathogens | Lymph nodes, white blood cells |
| Skeletal | Support and protection | Bones, cartilage |
| Muscular | Movement | Skeletal, smooth, cardiac muscle |
| Excretory | Remove waste from body | Kidneys, skin, lungs |

### Ecology
- **Producers** (autotrophs): make own food via photosynthesis (plants, algae)
- **Consumers** (heterotrophs): eat other organisms
  - Primary consumers: eat plants (herbivores)
  - Secondary consumers: eat herbivores (carnivores/omnivores)
  - Tertiary consumers: top predators
- **Decomposers**: break down dead matter (bacteria, fungi)
- Food chain: Sun → Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer → Decomposer
- **Biodiversity**: variety of species in an ecosystem; crucial for stability
- Threats to Philippine biodiversity: deforestation, coral bleaching, invasive species, overfishing

## CHEMISTRY

### Atomic Structure
- **Proton**: positive charge, in nucleus; atomic number = # protons
- **Neutron**: neutral, in nucleus; mass number = protons + neutrons
- **Electron**: negative charge, in orbitals around nucleus
- **Ion**: atom with unequal protons and electrons
  - Cation (+): lost electrons (metals)
  - Anion (-): gained electrons (nonmetals)
- **Isotopes**: same element, different number of neutrons

### Periodic Table Organization
- Periods (rows): 7 periods; elements in same period have same number of electron shells
- Groups/Families (columns): 18 groups; elements in same group have similar properties
- **Group 1** — Alkali metals: very reactive, soft, 1 valence electron
- **Group 2** — Alkaline earth metals: reactive, 2 valence electrons
- **Groups 3-12** — Transition metals
- **Group 17** — Halogens: very reactive nonmetals, 7 valence electrons
- **Group 18** — Noble gases: inert (very stable), 8 valence electrons (except He=2)

### Chemical Bonding
- **Ionic bond**: metal + nonmetal; electron TRANSFER; forms ions (NaCl, MgO)
- **Covalent bond**: nonmetal + nonmetal; electron SHARING; forms molecules (H₂O, CO₂, CH₄)
- **Metallic bond**: metal + metal; sea of electrons (explains conductivity)
- **Hydrogen bond**: weak attraction between δ+ H and δ- F, O, or N (important in water)

### Chemical Reactions
Balancing equations (Law of Conservation of Mass):
- Count atoms on each side; add coefficients to balance
- Example: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O (unbalanced)
  → 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O (balanced: 4H, 2O on each side)

Types of reactions:
1. **Synthesis**: A + B → AB (2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl)
2. **Decomposition**: AB → A + B (2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂)
3. **Single replacement**: A + BC → AC + B (Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂)
4. **Double replacement**: AB + CD → AD + CB (NaCl + AgNO₃ → AgCl + NaNO₃)
5. **Combustion**: fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O)

### Acids, Bases, and pH
- **Acid**: donates H⁺; pH < 7; sour taste; turns litmus red; reacts with metals
  - Examples: HCl (hydrochloric), H₂SO₄ (sulfuric), CH₃COOH (acetic/vinegar)
- **Base**: accepts H⁺ (donates OH⁻); pH > 7; bitter, slippery; turns litmus blue
  - Examples: NaOH (sodium hydroxide/lye), Ca(OH)₂ (lime), NH₃ (ammonia)
- **Neutral**: pH = 7 (pure water)
- pH scale: 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most basic)
- Neutralization: acid + base → salt + water (HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O)

### Gas Laws
- **Boyle's Law**: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ (pressure and volume are inversely proportional at constant T)
- **Charles' Law**: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ (volume and temperature are directly proportional at constant P)
- **Gay-Lussac's Law**: P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂ (pressure and temperature directly proportional at constant V)
- **Ideal Gas Law**: PV = nRT (P=pressure, V=volume, n=moles, R=gas constant, T=temperature)

## PHYSICS

### Kinematics (Motion)
Key equations (constant acceleration):
1. v = v₀ + at
2. d = v₀t + ½at²
3. v² = v₀² + 2ad
4. d = ½(v + v₀)t
Where: v=final velocity, v₀=initial velocity, a=acceleration, t=time, d=displacement

- **Speed**: distance/time (scalar)
- **Velocity**: displacement/time (vector — has direction)
- **Acceleration**: change in velocity / time (a = Δv/t)
- Free fall: a = g = 9.8 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s² (downward)

### Newton's Laws of Motion
1. **Inertia**: An object at rest stays at rest; in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by net force
2. **F = ma**: Force = mass × acceleration (SI unit: Newton = kg·m/s²)
3. **Action-Reaction**: For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction

### Work, Energy, Power
- **Work**: W = Fd cos θ (force × displacement × cos of angle between them)
  - Unit: Joule (J) = N·m
  - Work is done ONLY if displacement occurs in direction of force
- **Kinetic Energy**: KE = ½mv² (energy of motion)
- **Potential Energy** (gravitational): PE = mgh (h = height above reference)
- **Law of Conservation of Energy**: KE + PE = constant (in absence of friction)
- **Power**: P = W/t (rate of doing work); Unit: Watt (W) = J/s

### Electricity
- **Ohm's Law**: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  - V in volts (V), I in amperes (A), R in ohms (Ω)
- **Power**: P = IV = I²R = V²/R (watts)
- **Series circuit**: same current, resistances add: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ...
- **Parallel circuit**: same voltage, reciprocals add: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...

### Waves
- Wave equation: v = fλ (velocity = frequency × wavelength)
- **Electromagnetic spectrum** (shortest to longest wavelength):
  Gamma rays → X-rays → Ultraviolet → Visible light → Infrared → Microwaves → Radio waves
- Speed of light: c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s (in vacuum)
- **Sound waves**: mechanical; need medium; cannot travel in vacuum
  - Speed of sound in air ≈ 343 m/s at 20°C

## EARTH SCIENCE

### Philippine Geology
- Philippines is part of the **Ring of Fire** → frequent earthquakes and volcanic activity
- Major fault lines: Philippine Fault, Valley Fault System (near Metro Manila)
- **Active volcanoes**: Mayon (most active), Taal, Pinatubo (1991 eruption), Kanlaon, Bulusan
- **Pacific Plate** subducts under the **Philippine Plate** → causes earthquakes and volcanoes

### Rock Cycle
- **Igneous rocks**: formed from cooling magma/lava (granite = slow cooling; basalt = fast cooling)
- **Sedimentary rocks**: formed from compressed/cemented sediments (limestone, sandstone, shale)
- **Metamorphic rocks**: formed when existing rocks transform under heat/pressure (marble from limestone)

### Atmosphere and Climate
- Layers: Troposphere (weather) → Stratosphere (ozone layer) → Mesosphere → Thermosphere → Exosphere
- **Ozone layer**: in stratosphere; absorbs harmful UV radiation
- **Greenhouse gases**: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, H₂O vapor; trap heat → global warming
- Philippine climate types (4 types by PAGASA):
  - Type I: 2 distinct seasons (dry Nov-Apr, wet May-Oct) — Western coast
  - Type II: No dry season, very pronounced rainy season — Eastern coast (Samar, Leyte)
  - Type III: No pronounced max rain season — most of Mindanao
  - Type IV: Rainfall distributed evenly — interior areas

### Hydrosphere
- Water cycle: evaporation → condensation → precipitation → runoff/infiltration
- Coral Triangle: Philippines is in the "Coral Triangle" — highest marine biodiversity in the world
- Major bodies of water: Philippine Sea (east), South China Sea (Dagat Sinas, west), Sulu Sea, Celebes Sea

## PART 2: FILIPINO (Gramatika at Pagbabasa)

### Bahagi ng Pananalita (Parts of Speech)

**Pangngalan (Noun)**
- Karaniwan (common): tao, lungsod, bulaklak
- Pantangi (proper): Manila, Rizal, Pilipinas
- Abstrak (abstract): kalayaan, katarungan, pagmamahal
- Kolektibo (collective): kawan, grupo, lupon

**Panghalip (Pronoun) — Filipino Pronoun System**
| Panauhan | Nom. | Gen. | Obl. |
|---------|------|------|------|
| 1st sing. | ako | ko / akin | sa akin |
| 2nd sing. | ikaw/ka | mo / iyo | sa iyo |
| 3rd sing. | siya | niya / kanya | sa kanya |
| 1st pl. excl. (excl. you) | kami | namin / amin | sa amin |
| 1st pl. incl. (incl. you) | tayo | natin / atin | sa atin |
| 2nd pl. | kayo | ninyo / inyo | sa inyo |
| 3rd pl. | sila | nila / kanila | sa kanila |

**Pandiwa (Verb) — Aspeto (Aspect, not Tense)**
- **Pawatas** (infinitive): kumain, magluto, manood
- **Pangkasalukuyan** (in progress): kumakain, nagluluto, nanonood
- **Pangnakaraan** (completed): kumain, nagluto, nanood
- **Panghinaharap** (not yet): kakain, magluluto, manonood

**Pokus ng Pandiwa (Verb Focus)**
- **Aktor** focus (-um-, mag-): SINO ang gumalaw? → Kumain si Juan ng mansanas.
- **Layon** focus (-in, -an): ANO ang ginawa? → Kinain ni Juan ang mansanas.
- **Benepisyaryo** focus (-an): PARA/SAAN? → Nilutuan niya ako ng pagkain.
- **Kasangkapan** focus (i-): ANO ang ginamit? → Isinulat niya ang titik.

### Kayarian ng Salita (Word Structure)
- **Salitang-ugat**: root word without affixes (sulat, kain, takbo, luto)
- **Salitang may panlapi**: root + affixes
  - Unlapi (prefix): mag-, um-, na-, i- (mag**sulat**, u**m**akyat)
  - Gitlapi (infix): -um-, -in- (s**um**ulat, b**in**ili)
  - Hulapi (suffix): -an, -in, -han (sulat**an**, kain**in**)
  - Kabilaan (circumfix): i-...-an (i**sulat**an)

### Tayutay (Figure of Speech)
- **Simile**: paghahambing gamit ang "tulad ng" o "parang" → "Mabilis siyang parang kidlat"
- **Metapora**: direktang paghahambing → "Siya ay isang kidlat sa takbo"
- **Personipikasyon**: pagbibigay-buhay sa bagay/hayop → "Ang buwan ay ngumingiti sa gabi"
- **Pagmamalabis (Hyperbole)**: labis na pagpapalaki → "Natutulog na ang buong mundo"
- **Aliterasyon**: paulit-ulit na tunog → "Bawat bituin ay banaag ng bagong buhay"
- **Onomatopeya**: tunog na salita → "kaluskos, lagapak, haginit"

### Pagbabasa (Reading Comprehension in Filipino)
Key question types:
1. **Pangunahing Kaisipan**: What is the main idea? → Look for the overall message
2. **Detalye**: Specific facts stated in the text
3. **Implikasyon**: What is implied? Inference questions
4. **Layunin ng May-Akda**: Author's purpose (magbigay-impormasyon, manghikayat, maglibanin)
5. **Salita sa Konteksto**: Word meaning based on context

### Panitikang Pilipino (Philippine Literature)
Major works in Philippine literature:
- **José Rizal**: Noli Me Tangere, El Filibusterismo (novels exposing Spanish colonial abuses)
- **Francisco Balagtas**: Florante at Laura (pasyon style, Tagalog classic)
- **Amado V. Hernandez**: Mga Ibong Mandaragit (about labor struggle)
- **Nick Joaquin**: Portrait of the Artist as Filipino, A Heritage of Smallness
- **Carlos Bulosan**: America Is in the Heart (Filipino-American experience)

Literary periods:
1. Pre-colonial period (oral literature: epics, folk songs, riddles/bugtong, proverbs/salawikain)
2. Spanish colonial period (1565–1898): religious literature, corrido, awit, komedya
3. American colonial period (1898–1946): English as medium, nationalism in literature
4. Commonwealth period (1935–1944): deepening of nationalism
5. Japanese occupation (1941–1945): Filipino and vernacular literature promoted
6. Contemporary period (post-1946): diverse forms, English and Filipino side by side

### Salawikain (Proverbs) — Common in UPCAT
- "Aanhin pa ang damo kung patay na ang kabayo" — Too late to be useful
- "Kung hindi ukol, hindi bubukol" — If it's not meant to be, it won't happen
- "Ang hindi marunong lumingon sa pinanggalingan ay hindi makararating sa paroroonan" — Respect your roots to reach your destiny
- "Walang palaging tag-araw at walang palaging tag-ulan" — Nothing lasts forever
- "Nasa Diyos ang awa, nasa tao ang gawa" — God provides mercy, but humans must act

## SCIENCE PRACTICE QUESTIONS

**Biology Q1:** A cell has 46 chromosomes (2n=46). After meiosis, how many chromosomes will each daughter cell have?
Answer: **23** (haploid, n=23)

**Chemistry Q2:** Balance: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃
Answer: **4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃** (4 Fe, 6 O on each side)

**Physics Q3:** A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. What is its acceleration?
Answer: a = Δv/t = (20-0)/5 = **4 m/s²**

**Earth Science Q4:** The Philippines is located in the Ring of Fire. What does this explain?
Answer: Frequent earthquakes and volcanic eruptions due to tectonic plate boundaries

## FILIPINO PRACTICE QUESTIONS

**Q1:** Anong pokus ang ginagamit sa pangungusap: "Binili ni Anna ang sapatos"?
Answer: **Layon** (object focus; ang = patient/topic of sentence)

**Q2:** Ang "tulad ng dagat" ay halimbawa ng anong tayutay?
Answer: **Simile** (paghahambing gamit ang "tulad ng")

**Q3:** Anong aspeto ng pandiwa ang "nagsusulat"?
Answer: **Pangkasalukuyan** (in progress/ongoing action)
